About SWAC

The Society for the Welfare of Autistic Children (SWAC) was formed on February 3, 2000 by a group of very enthusiastic and devoted parents of individuals with autism. The aim of SWAC is to educate and train individuals with autism for their development to realise their fullest potential and improve the quality of their lives. SWAC is a pioneer non-profit, non-government, non-political and voluntary organization.

What is Autism?

Autism is a lifelong neuro-developmental disability, which affects the way a person communicates and relates to people around him. Individuals with autism are unable to relate to others in a meaningful way. Their ability to develop friendship is impaired as is their capacity to understand other peopleâ€™s feelings and thoughts. Individuals with autism can have accompanying learning disabilities but every one with the condition shares a difficulty in making sense of the world.

SWAC School for Autism

Society for the Welfare of Autistic Children(SWAC) is operating a special need school called SWAC School for Autism which provides intensive individualized instruction to children with autism, targeting the broad range of educational, sensory, prevocational, vocational, behavioral, social, communication, daily living, cognitive and motor dysfunctions that affect them, in a single integrated setting..
